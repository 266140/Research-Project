The Creative Process

of writing a novel can at times come off as tedious, time-consuming, and trite. Mostly, you just want to jump right into it, to just make up your story as it goes; I've been there. But one thing you have to understand is no good book, or novel, or movie (or any form of meida for that matter) is finished on the spot, a good piece of work is one with time, care and effort put into it, and all of that can be achieved by using the process this website is focused on.

As such, this website serves to break down said process in an easy-to-digest way, and I will include an example story called "RoughHouse" that progressivley gets developed with every step listed below so you can get real feeling for what ensues in each step.

Let's go!

Synopsis

In layman's terms, a synopsis is a story stripped down to its most basic and rawest form. It's nothing too detailed and serves mostly to plot an outline of your plot and characters. [1]

For example, a synopsis my story, RoughHouse, chornicles detective Lenard Claymore on his journey to defeat the 15 memebers of the Surly Kingpin gang before they can reach the city's sewer system, which they want to reach for reasons unknown to Lenard.

See what this synopsis does? It's simple! We've established the protagonist (Lenard), antagonist (Surly Kingpins), and the plot (Lenard must defeat the Surly Kingpins before the reach the sewer plant). You may notice that we've left some blank spaces open, but those are to be patched up in the next step!
